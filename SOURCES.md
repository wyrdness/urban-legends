## Online Resources & Databases

### Primary Resources
- **Snopes.com** (1994-present). https://www.snopes.com
  The definitive online resource for urban legend verification, rumor debunking, and folklore documentation.

- **International Society for Contemporary Legend Research (ISCLR)** 
  https://www.contemporarylegend.org
  Academic organization dedicated to contemporary legend study; publishes journal and maintains archives.

- **American Folklore Society** 
  https://www.afsnet.org
  Professional organization for folklore studies; resources and publications.

### Creepypasta & Internet Folklore
- **Creepypasta Wiki** 
  https://creepypasta.fandom.com
  Collaborative fiction/legend creation site; primary source for internet-native legends.

- **SCP Foundation** 
  http://www.scp-wiki.net
  Collaborative fiction project; demonstrates modern folklore creation mechanisms.

- **Reddit r/NoSleep** 
  https://www.reddit.com/r/nosleep
  Community for horror storytelling; source of modern legend variants.

### Archives
- **American Folklife Center, Library of Congress** 
  https://www.loc.gov/folklife
  Extensive folklore archives including urban legends.

- **Archive of American Folk Song** 
  Library of Congress collections.
